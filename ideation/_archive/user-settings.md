# User Settings & Configuration

## Business Configuration Settings

Users should be able to configure how the system operates based on their preferences and business philosophy.

## Business Principles & Models

Users can select which mental models and frameworks to apply:

**Proposed Options:**

### Strategic Frameworks
- **First Principles Thinking** - Break down problems to fundamental truths and build up from there
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** - Create uncontested market space rather than competing
- **Lean Startup** - Build-measure-learn feedback loops
- **Jobs to be Done** - Focus on what customers are trying to accomplish
- **Platform Thinking** - Build ecosystems and network effects
- **Design Thinking** - Human-centered problem solving
- **Systems Thinking** - Understanding interconnections and patterns
- **Agile Methodology** - Iterative development and flexibility

### Business Models
- **Bootstrapped** - Self-funded, sustainable growth
- **VC-Backed** - Venture capital funded, rapid growth
- **Subscription/SaaS** - Recurring revenue model
- **Marketplace** - Platform connecting buyers and sellers
- **Freemium** - Free basic tier, paid premium features
- **Direct-to-Consumer** - Sell directly without intermediaries

### Financial Approach
- **Revenue-First** - Focus on generating revenue early
- **Growth-First** - Focus on user acquisition and market share
- **Profit-First** - Emphasize profitability from day one
- **Efficiency-Focused** - Lean operations, minimal burn

## Mentor Personality

Users can select a mentor personality that influences how the AI guides them:

**Proposed Mentor Personalities:**

- **Elon Musk** - First principles thinking, ambitious vision, rapid execution
- **Steve Jobs** - Product perfection, user experience focus, reality distortion field
- **Jeff Bezos** - Customer obsession, long-term thinking, write memos not slides
- **Paul Graham** - Y Combinator wisdom, make something people want, talk to users
- **Naval Ravikant** - Leverage, specific knowledge, build wealth not status
- **Peter Thiel** - Contrarian thinking, monopoly strategy, 0 to 1 mindset
- **Sara Blakely** - Bootstrapping, persistence, resourcefulness
- **Reid Hoffman** - Network effects, blitzscaling, strategic relationships
- **Tim Ferriss** - Efficiency hacks, 80/20 principle, lifestyle design
- **Balanced Mentor** - Combination of best practices from multiple mentors

## AI Behavior Settings

- **Proactivity Level** - How proactive vs. reactive agents should be
- **Risk Tolerance** - Conservative vs. aggressive decision making
- **Communication Style** - Direct vs. detailed, formal vs. casual
- **Approval Frequency** - How often to ask for approval (low/medium/high)
- **Learning Mode** - How quickly to adapt to user's preferences

## Business Goals & Metrics

Users define what success looks like:
- Revenue targets
- User/customer targets
- Timeline preferences
- Work-life balance preferences
- Scale ambitions (solo business vs. building a team)
